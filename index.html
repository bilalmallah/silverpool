Our Services

Speech Sounds

Speech sound disorders are among the most common reasons children are referred for speech pathology. A child with a speech sound disorder may have difficulty pronouncing certain sounds, making it hard for others to understand them.

Our therapists assess each child's speech sound system and provide targeted intervention to help them produce sounds accurately and clearly.

  • Articulation disorders — difficulty producing specific sounds
  • Phonological disorders — patterns of sound errors
  • Childhood Apraxia of Speech (CAS)
  • Dysarthria and motor speech difficulties

Early intervention leads to the best outcomes for speech sound development.

Our Services

Stuttering

Stuttering is a speech disorder that affects the fluency of speech. It can involve repetitions of sounds, syllables or words, prolongations of sounds, and blocks or interruptions in speech.

Our therapists provide evidence-based treatment for stuttering using the Lidcombe Program and other proven approaches tailored to each child's needs.

  • Assessment of stuttering severity and impact
  • Lidcombe Program for young children
  • Smooth Speech and other fluency techniques
  • Strategies to manage anxiety and build confidence
  • Parent and family coaching for home support

The Lidcombe Program is a highly effective, evidence-based treatment for stuttering.

Our Services

Early Intervention

Early intervention is one of the most powerful tools available for supporting children with communication delays and disorders. The earlier a child receives support, the better the long-term outcomes.

We work with children from as young as 12 months, providing family-centred early intervention to support communication development.

  • Language delays and late talkers
  • Social communication difficulties
  • Speech sound delays in young children
  • Feeding and swallowing difficulties
  • Parent training and home program support

Early support before age 5 builds the strongest communication foundations.

Our Services

Expressive Language

Expressive language refers to the ability to use words, sentences, gestures and writing to communicate needs, thoughts and ideas. Children with expressive language difficulties may have a limited vocabulary, difficulty forming sentences or trouble expressing themselves clearly.

Individuals with expressive language disorder consistently find it difficult to express themselves more than other people their age. About 10 to 15 percent of children under age 3 have expressive language disorder.

  • Building vocabulary and word-finding skills
  • Improving sentence structure and grammar
  • Developing storytelling and narrative skills
  • Supporting written expression and literacy
  • Strategies for home and classroom use

With the right support, children with expressive language difficulties can thrive both socially and academically.

Our Services

Receptive Language

Receptive language is the ability to understand language. A child with receptive language difficulties may have trouble understanding what is said to them, following instructions, or comprehending what they read.

Our therapists use a range of evidence-based strategies to help children improve their understanding and processing of language in everyday situations.

  • Understanding and following instructions
  • Listening comprehension and memory
  • Understanding questions and concepts
  • Reading comprehension support
  • Classroom language and curriculum support

Receptive language is the foundation for learning — stronger understanding leads to stronger communication.

Our Services

Autism Spectrum Disorder

Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) is a neurodevelopmental condition that affects social communication, interaction and behaviour. Speech pathology plays a key role in supporting children with ASD to develop functional communication skills.

Our therapists offer a range of evidence-based interventions tailored to the individual child's profile, strengths and goals, working closely with families and schools.

  • Functional communication and AAC support
  • Social communication and pragmatic language
  • Play-based and naturalistic language therapy
  • PECS, Makaton and high-tech AAC devices
  • School readiness and transition support

Early diagnosis and intervention significantly improves long-term communication outcomes for children with ASD.

Our Services

Social Skills – Social Communication Disorder

Social communication disorder refers to persistent difficulties with the social use of verbal and nonverbal communication. Children may struggle to adjust their language to different situations, follow conversational rules, or understand implied meaning.

We provide targeted therapy to help children understand and use the social rules of communication, improve peer relationships and thrive in social settings.

  • Turn-taking and conversational skills
  • Understanding facial expressions and body language
  • Reading social cues and context
  • Group therapy and peer interaction practice
  • Strategies for home, school and community

Strong social communication skills open doors to friendships, learning and lifelong success.

Our Services

Alternative Augmentative Communication (AAC)

AAC refers to all the ways someone communicates besides talking — including signs, gestures, symbols, writing, and high-tech speech-generating devices. AAC supports individuals who have difficulty with verbal communication.

High-tech solutions include things like: Discussing an app on an iPad (iPad-based communication apps), using a dedicated speech-generating device, and more.

  • Low-tech AAC: PECS, communication boards, Makaton signing
  • High-tech AAC: iPad apps and speech-generating devices
  • Assessment to find the right AAC system for your child
  • Training for families, carers and school staff
  • Integration of AAC into daily routines and curriculum

AAC does NOT stop speech development — it supports and encourages it.

Our Services

Literacy

Literacy skills — reading, writing, and spelling — are closely linked to speech and language development. Speech pathologists are uniquely qualified to assess and treat literacy difficulties, particularly those related to phonological awareness.

We use structured, evidence-based literacy programs to support children with reading and writing difficulties, including dyslexia.

  • Phonological awareness and phonics skills
  • Reading fluency and comprehension
  • Spelling and written expression
  • Support for dyslexia and reading disorders
  • School curriculum and NAPLAN preparation support

Strong literacy foundations in early childhood lead to better academic outcomes across all subjects.
